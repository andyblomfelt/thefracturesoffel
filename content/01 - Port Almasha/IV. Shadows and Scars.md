Demiel was the first to arrive at breakfast. Pen scribbles filled the quiet, dewy air, only interrupted by the streaking of rags against glass as Uvi got the bar ready, and the light taps of a drizzle against the large windows overlooking the canal. He was so close to making progress on the encrypted note that fell out of the journal. It was consuming him. After all, he was good at puzzles. He figured out magic, he could figure this out.

Slowly, one by one, the rest of the group made their way to the table, unable to get him to respond with anything more than single words. The word "bookstore" snuck it's way into his head from the conversation happening outside his notes, and quickly agreed that it would be good to stop there before returning to the note. There had to be something here. Mateo's handwriting called to him.

---
Noro rounded the corner from her room, pondering if she should take Kaela up on her offer to go along the southern coast towards Nen'darei. This was the first family she had found in a long time. However broken, it was something. Was it worth leaving for a chance to go back?

"Did you hear Mei come back at all last night, Noro?" The booming voice of Trusto snapped her out of it.

"I... have not seen her at all. The last time I saw her was at Layla's night before last." She gave a coarse purr, still working through the morning.

Trusto continued to speak his concerns; Mei was usually better about letting him know before she left town. Noro, being as connected as she was through the city, offered to put some feelers out as they had some errands in town today anyway.

Kaela's offer continued to weigh on her, even during this conversation. She brought it up to Trusto, wanting his opinion if it was worth it to leave given how strapped he was at the Gallery.

Noro furrowed her brow, "I would hate to be away from home if something happens. If things go south..." She paused, remembering the magical surge that put a target on their backs 24 hours ago. "Are you able to get your hands on a pair of sending stones? It might be good to keep in touch during the journey."

Trusto sighed, the possibility of something happening was more likely than he was comfortable with.

"Let me talk to Halur."

"So, anywhere else other than The Vidya?" Noro called out as the five of them make their way towards Ismir Market, the drizzle lightly coating their clothes as awnings were drawn on either side of the street.

"*She is named Uriel. Her power is stolen. You were right.*"

The group immediately recognized the church they discovered the day before. Uriel, Tiyeme explained, was framed as the God of Judgements and Justice. Her word was always the final say in the world. Matters of judgement were attributed to her. What her name is doing in this note, however, was unclear still.

"I will be honest, I don't know what to make of that." Noro replied after thinking for a while.

## Empty Promises
The stained-glass tiles hanging above Ismir Market cast a vibrant display of colorful light on the water snaking it's way between shop stands. As the five travelers weaved their way through disorganized streets, the crowd of traders slowly began to trickle into life. It was easy to be anonymous in this part of town.

Most buildings were made of a brown-washed limestone carved into simple shapes, so a wooden building taking up a major corner of the market was certainly out of place. A wooden hanging sign gently waved back and forth from the wind sneaking between the ceiling tiles: a single "V" surrounded by a sun. The carving was simple, unassuming, a symbol of status in a market full of traders working so hard to pull attention. Noro walked in with purpose.

The double doors opened to a kind of study room. Five long tables stretched through the main corridor, and bookshelves lined the walls, only separated by ornate glasswork peeking into the market outdoors. The windows weren't made of stained glass, but the light sneaking in through the market certainly created a similar effect.

"I'll be dead in the ground before I make another deal with you, Kaveh!" A voice boomed from one of the central tables, where a man in yellow robes dawning a thick, stubby beard slammed his fist down as he stood up. The man across from him shifted back on the bench, his black collared shirt falling to either side of his body loosely, as he cupped his forehead with his hand.

Tiyeme's shoulder was gruffly bumped as the man in yellow robes stormed out, not willing to wait for the ones standing in the doorway to get out of his way. The market buzzed at the opening of the door, and fell silent once again as it closed behind him.

Tiyeme quickly sat in front of the presumed Kaveh, unfazed at the dramatic exit.

"Hello, fair traveller! My friends and I are looking for a map of the Nyrian Sea. Can you point us in the right direction"

Kaveh's hand lifted to reveal a confused look.

"I'm sorry, who are you?"

The two conversed for a bit, while the others browsed the offerings of the Vidya. It seemed to be a location meant more for legal battles than the sale of books. Almashan contract law, ship manifests, and other documents filled the old bookshelves. 

Kaveh, eventually revealing his true colors as a salesman with a sharp tongue, showed Tiyeme around the store, offering so much and so little at the same time.

"Have you ever been to Dureya? Lu'xai? Let me tell you, it's absolutely incredible. If you're headed there, you must bring some of my wares with you."

Ewan, reading a book and listening in, sneered to himself. This man lied as easily as he breathed, and Tiyeme was eating it up.

Noro, Demiel, and Ylva continued onwards to the front desk, inquiring about maps. A young woman introducing herself as Yali offered what help she could.

"Well, we don't sell maps directly, that's more the business of the trade commission." She brushed a hand through her hair, sizing up the group and Noro's smooth talking. 

"However, we might have a service that could be beneficial to you. Come with me."

The three are led to a small "reading room" in the back. No windows. Lit only by a single lamp in the corner nestled next to a potted plant. Electric lights weren't unheard of in Almasha—The Arcanum did allow for approved magical batteries for powering simple devices like this. The price, however, was steep, and bringing their eye to your work was a risk only some were willing to take.

"You certainly know the right things to ask, cat." She praised Noro. "We at the Vidya do offer a service of forging manifest documents in order to get into the trade commission's records room. It's an easy task, their operation is built more on intimidation than actual process."

"Anun, could you come help our new friends?"

A deal was struck to create a forged manifest. 25 gold later, and a manifest of unlisted goods headed to the Nyrian Sea would be ready within the hour. Noro thanked them in her usual smooth demeanor, and returned to see Ewan speaking with Kaveh at one of the tables.

"So," Ewan sighed with exhaustion, "am I to assume you don't have any actual wares with you?"

"Well, we make to order! You must understand, the quality is unmatched. I could have a shipment here from Lu'xai within 3 days. Trust me, a blade forged with the salts of Dureya is something worth the wait." Kaveh spoke bombastically, his pitch rolling off the tongue effortlessly. 

Ewan blinked in exasperation.

"You're not worth my time. We're leaving, Tiyeme."

## Misir's Trinkets
The five continued down their list of planned stops, weaving their way towards the south-eastern part of Ismir market towards Old Almasha where the Serpent and the Bloom was waiting. Just before the ceiling tiles ended and the dreary sky took over, a slight sparkle from a counter to the left caught Ylva's eye.

The limewashed building was no more than 2 stories tall, the stained glass cutting the building off before it's entire size could be noticed. A large, open serving counter extended most of the length of the facade, where three clerks were showing off jewelry of all kinds. Bamboo tiles clanked above, hanging from the counter, with a few letters carved into each: MISIR'S.

Ewan and Tiyeme stayed behind, while Demiel and Noro ran to catch up with Ylva, who was already entranced by the display of various pieces on the counter.

"My those horns are beautiful!" An employee in a brown vest and white shirt walked over, her short blonde hair falling just above the shoulders, "Can I interest you in some accessories for them? We can custom fit any piece to fit within the hour..." her voice trailed off.

"Is Misir in today, love? I bring word from Trusto." Noro interrupted

"Well..." the clerk looked as if she was waiting for Noro to say something more, but nothing came.

"I'm sorry, I think she's with a client today. You might check back at the end of the day?"

Disappointed, Noro turned to leave, before she heard Ylva pipe up suddenly "Well, this ring is beautiful, but I hear the price of silver has been going up." Noro and Demiel looked at each other briefly, confused as to the sudden change in tone from Ylva.

"Oh, well why didn't you start with that!" The clerk's demeanor suddenly changed. "That is certainly a common trend, but here we find that the value is in the setting. Would you like a demonstration?" Her hand gestured to the side door around the corner, inviting the group in.

Noro and Demiel looked at each other, then back at Ewan and Tiyeme, who seemed to be discussing their own deal with an unfamiliar man dealing animal companions. Presuming that they were good for a while, the three continued on towards the side door.

Noro leaned into Ylva's ear, "Where did you learn that one?"

"Oh, I overheard someone else," she coyly resonded.

The side door opened into the main room of Misir's Trinkets, where the open counter looked out to the market on the left, and small worktables on the right. Jewelers of various races and backgrounds had loupes pulled over their eyes with embedded glowing gems, the only source of illumination in this room. 

Another door opened on the opposite side of the room, where a woman with thick black hair just above her shoulder adjusted a blue and yellow jacket. She gestured to come in, and closed the door behind them.

Her office was well-used. Metals and gems were scattered around the room onto various workstations and bookshelves nonsensically, but it seemed like she could identify where anything was here.

"So," she sat down at her center desk in front of a stand meant to rest a forearm, "who's up first?"

The three blinked at each other for a moment.

"I'll go" Ylva piped up. She sat down and placed her forearm in the stand as directed by who was presumably Misir. She procured a small set of tools and continued her explanation.

"Well, usually I charge around 300 gold for a band removal, but since you're a friend of Trusto's, let's make it 200. Sound good?"

Ylva procured the gold almost instantly.

"Well, easy enough then. So what's Trusto up to these days?" She turned to Noro.

"Ah, good good," she purred in response, "I actually had a question for you Misir, if you don't mind."

She continued as Misir started prodding at Ylva's suppression band "I know we haven't had the pleasure of meeting, but my associate Mei often comes by looking for information. I'm wondering if you've seen her recently?"

Misir stopped working for just a moment and leaned back in her chair, placing one of the tools on her chin in thought.

"You know, I hadn't even realized it, but it has been a bit since she's been by." She leaned forward again to continue her work. "I believe she stopped by a few days ago asking if I had seen anyone with connections to the Scars of Calbraithe. Didn't know you all did dealings with them."

Noro and Demiel pulled up a chair to watch her expertise.

"Oh no, we don't deal with them," Noro clarified, "but she was looking into them. Not to pry, but *have* you helped anyone associated with the Scars?"

Suddenly, the suppression band on Ylva's wrist gave a faint *click*, and fell onto the table below.

"Yes, just the other day. I don't remember his name, but I believe it started with a T. And his boots were so fucking loud, it distracted me."

She turned to Ylva

"Now, that should be good to go. Keep in mind that not having one on might bring the Arcanum sniffing, but not my problem if you can't keep that to yourself."

She placed her elbows on the table and leaned in.

"If you make it my problem, you'll regret it."

## The Contingency

It took the better part of 30 minutes for Tiyeme and Ewan to traverse the entirety of the Az'mari Court, the government complex of Port Almasha. They walked along the side of courtyards that framed the facades of shorter buildings, but still grand with large glass domes and arched windows. Crowds of people in brown suits and other robes intersected their path, likely leaving their workday at the various Commissions of Almasha.

On the easternmost edge of the Court sat a building significantly taller than the others. The rectangular building sported multiple balconies overlooking the docks to the east, still buzzing with activity as trade waits for no one. An ornate trim circled the top edge of the building, with the words "Almashan Trade Commission" embedded in the art.

Ewan pushed open one of the sets of double doors leading inside, and a large, circular counter awaited them in a large, dramatic room adorned with beautiful marble floors.

"Welcome to the trade commission, can I get your country of origin please?" A deep voice called as they approached the counter. A man with gray skin and horns stood slightly taller than them, hand outstretched.

"Good evening! I hope the day's trade has treated you well." Tiyeme confidently walked up, handing over the forged manifest.

"Headed to Nen'darei, are you? Good luck, I hear the Nyrian Sea is not the kindest this time of year."

He folded the paper and handed it back to Tiyeme.

"Any navigational charts you might need are upstairs." He pointed to the grand staircase ascending to large, wooden double doors. "Someone up there can help you find exactly what you need, but looks like everything has already been paid on your goods."

---
The two ascended the staircase and entered a large library with a bronze globe in the center, clearly showing the world of Fel in all it's glory. They were quickly helped by a short woman in deep blue robes, who pointed them towards the section related to the Southern Sea on the right.

Tiyeme and Ewan scoured the shelves, and found more detail than they expected. Instead of a single map of the area, Tiyeme pulled out a book bearing a title Ewan recognized: *The HMCS Marrow Gleam*.

They quickly flipped through the book. This was the log of an old Calbraithen ship, that travelled through the Nyrian Sea. The charts were immensely detailed, showing the vessel's journey through the southern seas.

As Tiyeme copied the map into his own notes with expert precision, Ewan felt a nagging in his head. The Marrow Gleam was meant to be stationed in Port Almasha during the annexation. In fact, there's no reason *any* Calbraithen ship would've gone further south than here.

Finishing up his work, Tiyeme went to close the book, but stopped at a folded note at the back. While Ewan searched for other relevant charts, Tiyeme opened the note to discover a personal note to the Captain of the Marrow Gleam.

He frantically read through it while Ewan was distracted.

"What's that you got there?" Ewan suddenly spoke from behind.

Tiyeme, his face pale, turned and offered a panicked, unconvincing smile. "Oh, nothing at all, the map is ready! Let's join the others." He started to walk away.

"You're hiding something," Ewan said, his voice flat and unyielding. The words seemed to stop time, waiting for someone to admit defeat first.

Tiyeme slowly turned around, unfolding the note to hide the parts he knew would hurt Ewan the most. The words "Paladins of Calbraithe" reflected in his eyes like fire. His hand shot out to grab the note.

Duncan's name echoed through the silence. Ewan’s face hardened, and left without a word.

## A Wordless Goodbye

*(10th of Hostar, 721 A.C.)*

Ewan woke up before the others, the creak of the door of his new, private room echoing through the halls. It felt strange to not be going back home today, but the weight of his discoveries the day before hung like a storm over his head.

He stopped for a moment. Demiel's door was wide open.

The sheets were folded nicely, and nothing was left. He obviously returned at some point last night. Out of curiosity, and maybe a little sympathy for the events of the night before, Ewan stepped in to find a single note sitting next to a lit lantern:

*Hello everyone,* 

*While it has been a pleasure traveling and working with you all (at times), I regret to inform you that I will be moving on. I fear this business you all have gotten up to is simply a bit much for me, not to mention beyond my capabilities and expertise. I would prefer if you do not seek me out too soon, as I need a bit of space. For the time being, I plan to head to the Arcanum in search of employment, so if you should need me in the future, you will hopefully find me there – though I will be quite busy with my studies assuming all goes well. I wish you all success in your endeavors and in your travels to Nen’darei.* 

*Best regards,* 
*Dr. Demiel Wh–

The note cut off halfway through his name, a splattering of ink suggesting he left in a hurry. Ewan sighed, and looked up from the note, where he noticed one of the drapes by the window wave slightly, unprompted. The door shifted slightly, as if someone had walked out. 

He gave a slight smile. *Bastard never could have a straightforward conversation*, he thought to himself.
