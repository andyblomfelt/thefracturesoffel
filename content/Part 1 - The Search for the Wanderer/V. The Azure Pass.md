###### Session 13 - September 7th, 2025
## Distant Celebration

*(10th of Hostar, 721 A.C.)*

The first day on the water passed rather uneventfully. The new crew of the Line Six tended to their duties, and spent their meals playing cards over the simple foods acquired by Noro. Clouds stretched low and gray across the sky, keeping the sun away for most of the journey. Every so often, the remnants of storms that had lost their strength at the edge of the pass pressed in, washing the deck with stray curtains of rain before moving on.

Later that night, with the sun completely set and the last bits of sunlight tinting the sky, Ylva walked onto the flybridge balcony to find Tryf away from his post for the first time since they departed. A drink in his hands, he leaned over the balcony towards the port side of the ship, looking out at whatever city sat on the Azure coast a few miles away. Large, pale yellow archways extended out into the water, holding up the extravagant buildings that made up this city. Detail was hard to make out at this distance, but as nighttime lights started to turn on, a distinctive pair of towers protruded from the right side of the skyline, coming to a flat roof that seemed to be lit from underneath. Palm trees cast dancing shadows against the large walls of these towers.

Ylva walked up and stood next to him. His eyes stayed fixed to the coast, only acknowledging her presence with a soft smile.

A faint whistling sound from the city caught her attention, thanks to the heightened senses of Hjordis. Ylva turned her head to see what in the city caused this sound, and was suddenly bilded by a brilliantly bright display of reds and yellows lighting up the sky. Fireworks, one after the other, exploded and resonated in their chests.

- Ship passes by Nargess off in the distance that night, where a fireworks display starts. Tryf and Ylva discuss for a while, and learn that they're both from small towns.
- During this display, noro is playing a little flute on the edge of the concrete chimney of the ship. Lance tries to join in with his lute. Noro is initially apprehensive, but eventually says it's ok for him to join. 
- Ewan and Kaela have a private conversation in the map room during this. Ewan pays Kaela to make sure they spend a few extra days in Nen'darei Point. Kaela explains that they'll have to pass through it eventually before continuing to follow the leyline on foot. She is in a hurry, and can't offer more than a day.
- Everyone meets on the balcony. Ylva notices a coded message in the lighthouse at the point ahead of Nargess. Noro decodes it from Thieve's Cant, saying there's an unnatural storm ahead and to use caution.
- Group eventually decides to move at a more stealthy pace from then on, to avoid whatever is coming.

## Suspicious Herds

*(12th of Hostar, 721 A.C.)*

- After a couple more days of travel, Ylva spots a ship far on the horizon stuck in ice. Odd because these are warmer waters, even during colder months like this. 
- On the other side of the ship as they're looking for this, a deer walking on water is just standing there.
- Ylva talks to the deer, trying to figure out what's going on. Apparently, it just walks out here for the kelp that comes to the surface. Eventually notice a herd of 8 or so deer just munching on kelp, standing on water as if it's ground.
- Bird suddenly descends from the cloudy sky at supersonic speed, yanking the deer underwater violently in a flash of black.
- After a moment, a second one hits the water on the stern. Then a third, and a fourth, until there are hundreds of birds diving from the clouds above (this is a skill challenge for the party)
- Party eventually succeeds the skill challenge, just barely. Tiyeme and Ewan are severely hurt, and the flybridge has sustained damage. The ship can still move, however.
- Ylva dresses the carcasses of the ones that hit the ship effortlessly, and serves up dinner with it.
- Ewan checks on the engine, and while it's still intact, their fuel reserves will just barely get them to Istali.
