# 9th of Hostar, 721 A.C.
###### Session 09 - August 4th, 2025 (continued)

Demiel was the first to arrive at breakfast. Pen scribbles filled the quiet, dewy air, only interrupted by the streaking of rags against glass as Uvi got the bar ready, and the light taps of a drizzle against the large windows overlooking the canal. He was so close to making progress on the encrypted note that fell out of the journal. It was consuming him. After all, he was good at puzzles. He figured out magic, he could figure this out.

Slowly, one by one, the rest of the group made their way to the table, unable to get him to respond with anything more than single words. The word "bookstore" snuck it's way into his head from the conversation happening outside his notes, and quickly agreed that it would be good to stop there before returning to the note. There had to be something here. Mateo's handwriting called to him.

---
Noro rounded the corner from her room, pondering if she should take Kaela up on her offer to go along the southern coast towards Nen'darei. This was the first family she had found in a long time. However broken, it was something. Was it worth leaving for a chance to go back?

"Did you hear Mei come back at all last night, Noro?" The booming voice of Trusto snapped her out of it.

"I... have not seen her at all. The last time I saw her was at Layla's night before last." She gave a coarse purr, still working through the morning.

Trusto continued to speak his concerns; Mei was usually better about letting him know before she left town. Noro, being as connected as she was through the city, offered to put some feelers out as they had some errands in town today anyway.

Kaela's offer continued to weigh on her, even during this conversation. She brought it up to Trusto, wanting his opinion if it was worth it to leave given how strapped he was at the Gallery.

Noro furrowed her brow, "I would hate to be away from home if something happens. If things go south..." She paused, remembering the magical surge that put a target on their backs 24 hours ago. "Are you able to get your hands on a pair of sending stones? It might be good to keep in touch during the journey."

Trusto sighed, the possibility of something happening was more likely than he was comfortable with.

"Let me talk to Halur."

---
"So, anywhere else other than The Vidya?" Noro calls out as the five of them make their way towards Ismir Market, the drizzle lightly coating their clothes as awnings are being drawn around the street. 

"Alchemist? Pharmacy?" Ewan raised in response. "Maybe a liquor store?"

"Sounds good to me, there are a few places that I know. What about you Demiel?" She turned to face him.

"Demiel?" She poked his shoulder, not getting a response.

A few seconds pass of awkward silence before Demiel looks up suddenly from his book, making eye contact for the first time in what feels like days. 

"I just solved it."

The group stopped in their tracks. 

"*She is named Uriel. Her power is stolen. You were right.*"

The group immediately recognizes the church they discovered the day before. Uriel, Tiyeme explained, was framed as the God of Judgements and Justice. Her word was always the final say in the world. Matters of judgement were attributed to her. What her name is doing in this note, however, was unclear still.

"I will be honest, I don't know what to make of that." Noro replied after thinking for a while.
